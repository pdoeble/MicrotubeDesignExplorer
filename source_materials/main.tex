\abstract{%
In automotive or aerospace contexts, air--liquid hollow-fiber heat exchangers (HFHX)
offer a high-area tube-bundle architecture for compact mobile cooling systems,
but metallic microtube bundles remain costly and manufacturing-intensive.
Polymer fibers may reduce tube-supply cost and mass, yet their low wall
conductivity, pressure integrity, and manufacturing tolerances must be evaluated
together rather than through surface-area arguments alone. In the sub-millimeter
range, the standard conductivity objection to polyamide (PA) loses quantitative
force: when outer convective resistance dominates, the PA wall contributes only
about $2$--$6\,\%$ of total thermal resistance at the material-specific minimum
wall ($t_{\mathrm{min},\mathrm{PA}}=0.025\,\mathrm{mm}$).
This paper tests whether that local compensation survives package and
manufacturing constraints.
A resistance-based design-space sweep over $(d_{\mathrm{o}},t)$ uses VDI Heat
Atlas G1/G7 correlations anchored to an aluminum benchmark and converts the
local heat-transfer coefficient to package-scaled conductance
$k_{\mathrm{o}}A_{\mathrm{o}}$.
Six constraints are applied to both materials: minimum wall thickness, coolant
throughput, straight-tube friction pressure drop, derated burst pressure,
capillary rise in the potting zone, and a preliminary tube-supply-cost cutoff.
The resulting design-boundary map shows that, under the normal extrusion
tolerance, a conditional sub-millimeter PA prototype target remains near
$d_{\mathrm{o}}\approx 0.35$--$0.65\,\mathrm{mm}$ at wall thicknesses
moderately above the process minimum.
The corresponding aluminum minimum-wall option is feasible only from
$d_{\mathrm{o}}\approx 0.63$--$0.65\,\mathrm{mm}$ because the example
tube-supply-cost cutoff excludes smaller aluminum diameters under the assumed
benchmark pitch.
The PA interval is therefore a model-derived prototype target that still
requires tests of wall tolerances, potting geometry, and bundle durability.
\keywords{hollow-fiber heat exchangers, polyamide microtubes,
tube-bank heat transfer, VDI Heat Atlas, design-space constraints,
potting capillarity}%
}
